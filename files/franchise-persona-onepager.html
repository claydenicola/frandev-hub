The Woman We're Looking to Grow Alongside

Mainstream Boutique · Ideal Franchise Owner

"Our franchisees don't just sell clothes — they build relationships, create confidence, and become the heart of their neighborhoods."

Who she is

She believes, like we do, that women deserve to feel extraordinary every single day — and she's decided she wants to deliver on that belief in her own community, under her own shingle, with our blueprint behind her.

She's at an inflection point. Her career has given her most of what it's going to. Her kids are more independent. Maybe she's built and sold something before — maybe even owned a franchise. Or maybe she's finally at the point where it's time to work for herself. She's always wanted to make the leap, but the fear held her back. Not anymore.

When she walks into a room, people know her and often compliment her on her outfit, asking where it's from. She has the style, the experience, the network, and the drive — and she's ready for her next chapter.

For many of our owners, this is a family endeavor. Mother-daughter teams from day one. Or moms building something now that their daughters will step into and carry forward.

She does her homework. She brings her spouse into the decision. She researches, takes her time. And when she commits, she commits fully.

What she brings

  • Style & PresenceTaste is the one thing we can't teach, and she has it naturally.
  • CommunityShe's already woven into her town — church, school, businesses, women's groups. She has a room she can fill.
  • ExperienceOften in fashion, retail, sales, management, or running her own business. Fashion or business-ownership experience is ideal, but not required.
  • LeadershipShe's built and led teams before. She knows how to delegate, how to hold a standard, how to create a culture.
  • Commitment to the BlueprintShe wants our system because it works. She's not looking to reinvent the model.
  • CapitalMinimum $80,000 liquid and $250,000 net worth to start the conversation. Our top performers tend to sit around $250k household income and $1.25M net worth — but the financial picture grows alongside the business.

What ownership looks like

Hands-on, especially through year one. The strongest owners are in their store building the team and the customer relationships from day one.

Owner-operator or semi-absentee. Both are welcome. Semi-absentee owners bring a strong on-site leader — typically an Operating Partner with equity — to carry the brand standard day to day.

Built for multi-unit growth. Increasingly, owners begin with a development plan — two, three, or more locations mapped at signing. Single-unit ownership is still available and fully supported, but our Scale With Style Program is designed for owners who see the long game and want to scale from day one.

Who this isn't for

  • Someone looking for a passive financial investment with no operating role
  • Someone who wants to change the Mainstream Boutique model instead of execute it
  • Someone without a real community network in their target market
  • Someone unprepared to be in the store, hands-on, through the first year
